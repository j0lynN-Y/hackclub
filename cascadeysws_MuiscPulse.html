MusicPulse shows how 2, 3, 4 and 5 notes fit into 1 beat.

This is currently running at 120bpm!

  • Crotchets have 1 note in 1 beat
  • Quavers have 2 notes in 1 beat
  • Triplets have 3 notes in 1 beat
  • Semiquavers have 4 notes in 1 beat
  • Quintuplets have 5 notes in 1 beat
  • Sextuplets have 6 notes in 1 beat

    • Here are some cool facts about the different note durations!

    Crotchet or Quarter Note

  • It is commonly used to establish the basic beat in a piece of music.
  • For example, in Twinkle Twinkle Little Star, the steady beat is a perfect example of crotchets.

    • Quaver or Eighth Note

  • It is often used to create a faster rhythm.
  • For example, in Beethoven's Fur Elise, the opening motif uses alternating quavers.

    • Semiquaver or Sixteenth Note

  • It is often used to create intricate and quick rhythmic patterns.
  • For example, in Vivaldi's Spring from The Four Seasons, the violin part has rapid semiquavers.

    • Triplets

  • It adds a feeling of movement and swing.
  • For example, in Chopin's Minute Waltz, the triplets provide motion and elegance.

    • Quintuplets

  • It is often used for complex, unconventional rhythms.
  • For example, in Holst's The Planets - Mars, the Bringer of War, quintuplets contribute to the irregular warlike rhythm.

    • Sextuplets

  • Creates rapid flowing phrases.
  • For example, in Franz Liszt's Hungarian Rhapsody No. 2, sextuplets make very virtuosic rapid passages.
